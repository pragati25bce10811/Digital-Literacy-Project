# Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests. It usually begins with a scammer contacting the victim through a phone call, message, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or a trusted person.

The scammer may send a fake payment request or a malicious link and ask the user to click on it or enter their UPI PIN. In some cases, they claim that money will be credited, but instead, the user ends up approving a payment. Once the victim shares their details or enters the PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

College students and people who are new to digital payments are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information.

Another important aspect of UPI fraud is the misuse of fake apps and screen-sharing techniques. Scammers may ask users to install unknown applications or share their screen, allowing them to access confidential information. These tactics make it easier for attackers to gain control over financial transactions without the victim realizing it.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment platforms. Users should never share their UPI PIN, OTP, or click on unknown links. Being alert and informed is the best way to prevent such cybercrimes.
